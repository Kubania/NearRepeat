Summary

A significant and meaningful near repeat victimization pattern found!

After an incident, there is evidence of an over-representation of events in the local area for a certain amount of time. There may be crime prevention value in addressing this pattern. Details:
Within 1 to 100 meters of an initial incident, near repeats are overrepresented for up to 4 days.
Within 101 to 200 meters of an initial incident, near repeats are overrepresented for up to 4 days.
Within 201 to 300 meters of an initial incident, near repeats are overrepresented for up to 2 days.

How big is the difference? In the immediate space-time vicinity to a source event, the most over-represented space-time range that is significant is the zone from 1 to 100 meters and from 0 to 2 days from an initial incident. The chance of another incident is about 168 percent greater than if there were no discernible pattern.

A significant and meaningful repeat victimization pattern found. After an incident, there is evidence of an over-representation of events at the same place up to 4 days after an initial incident. The most over-represented repeat victimization range that is significant is the zone from 0 to 2 days from an initial incident. The chance of another incident is about 1460 percent greater than if there were no repeat victimization pattern.
